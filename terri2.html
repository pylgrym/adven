DUNGEON OF DESPAIR
An Adventure in 50 Puzzles  |  Complete Walkthrough & Transcript
Infocom-Compatible  ·  DESPAIR v1.03  ·  1984  ·  Softronix Inc.
Walkthrough compiled by: Frustrated Gamer  ·  CompuServe Forum Post #4471
⚠ WARNING — READ BEFORE PLAYING This walkthrough exists because DUNGEON OF DESPAIR is, without question, the worst text adventure ever published. I have spent 340 hours on this game. My marriage has suffered. My cat no longer trusts me. The puzzles are not "difficult" — they are "broken" or "insane" or "authored by someone who hates you personally." Every solution in this document was discovered either by accident, by exhaustive verb-spamming, or by bribing the programmer's nephew at a convention. You have been warned. Type along if you must.

Area 1: The Starting Cottage

Puzzles 1–9
PUZZLE 001
The Front Door verb issue
You are standing outside a cottage. There is a door to the north. A welcome mat lies at your feet.
OPEN DOOR, ENTER DOOR, GO NORTH, KNOCK DOOR, USE DOOR, PUSH DOOR, TURN HANDLE, PULL DOOR
NOTE: The door is not locked. It is not stuck. The game simply does not understand the word OPEN in this context because the programmer forgot to include the synonym table for exterior doors. The only verb that works here is VISIT.
VISIT DOOR
You walk up and visit the door. It swings open as if delighted to receive company.
PUZZLE 002
The Welcome Mat invisible item
Cottage exterior. A welcome mat is here.
NOTE: You must take the welcome mat. There is no reason given. It becomes important in Puzzle 37. If you do not take it now, you cannot finish the game and there is no way to go back.
TAKE MAT
Taken. The mat smells faintly of regret.
PUZZLE 003
The Cottage Interior — Locked Chest moon logic
You are inside the cottage. There is a wooden chest here. On the mantle sits a candle. A grandfather clock ticks in the corner.
OPEN CHEST, UNLOCK CHEST, EXAMINE CHEST, BREAK CHEST, PICK LOCK
You tried to kick the chest. The chest kicks back. You are dead.
NOTE: The chest opens at exactly 3:00. The clock must be set to 3:00 first. There is no in-game clue for this. The clock currently reads 7:42.
EXAMINE CLOCK
A grandfather clock. It reads 7:42. It has hands.
SET CLOCK TO THREE
You set the clock to 3:00. A distant chime sounds. The chest clicks open.
OPEN CHEST
The chest is open. Inside is a brass key and a note reading "DO NOT USE THE KEY."
TAKE KEY
Taken.
NOTE: Use the key. Ignore the note. The note is a lie. If you obey the note, you will reach Puzzle 22 with no key and softlock the game. The programmer thought this was funny. It is not funny.
PUZZLE 004
The Candle timing puzzle
Cottage interior. The candle on the mantle is unlit.
LIGHT CANDLE, TAKE CANDLE, BURN CANDLE, IGNITE CANDLE
NOTE: You have no matches or lighter. The game will not tell you this. You must sneeze on the candle. This is established exactly nowhere in the fiction.
SNEEZE
You sneeze. The candle, mysteriously, ignites.
TAKE CANDLE
You take the lit candle. You will need this. DO NOT PUT IT DOWN OR IT GOES OUT FOREVER.
PUZZLE 005
The Trapdoor hidden exit
Cottage interior. The room description does not mention a trapdoor.
GO EAST, GO WEST, GO SOUTH, EXAMINE FLOOR, SEARCH ROOM
NOTE: The trapdoor exists. EXAMINE RUG to find it. The rug is not mentioned in the room description. The game does not know the word LOOK UNDER but it does know EXAMINE.
EXAMINE RUG
Under the rug (which you apparently moved with your mind) is a trapdoor.
OPEN TRAPDOOR
The trapdoor swings open, revealing a ladder going down.
GO DOWN
You descend into the cellar.
PUZZLE 006
The Dark Cellar light puzzle
It is pitch black. You are likely to be eaten by a grue. Or perhaps a "groo." The game spells it differently each time.
NOTE: You have the candle. This should work. It does not work because the game requires you to explicitly HOLD UP CANDLE before it counts as a light source.
HOLD UP CANDLE
You hold the candle aloft. The cellar is revealed. A barrel, a spider, and an inexplicable filing cabinet are here.
PUZZLE 007
The Filing Cabinet anachronism puzzle
Cellar. A filing cabinet stands against the wall. The game is set in medieval times.
OPEN CABINET, SEARCH CABINET, EXAMINE CABINET, READ CABINET
NOTE: The filing cabinet requires a four-digit PIN. You learn the PIN by reading the back of the box the game came in. The PIN is 1138. If you don't have the box, I'm sorry. The PIN is 1138.
ENTER 1138
The cabinet clicks open. Inside is a silver whistle and a document labeled "PLOT."
TAKE WHISTLE
Taken.
READ DOCUMENT
The document reads: "You are a hero. There is a dungeon. Go to the dungeon. Good luck." This is, in its entirety, the game's plot.
PUZZLE 008
The Barrel red herring
Cellar. A large barrel dominates the room.
NOTE: The barrel contains wine. The wine does nothing. You can drink it, which wastes two turns. Do not interact with the barrel at all. I wasted eleven hours on the barrel. There is nothing in or about the barrel that matters. I am including it here as a warning to others.
IGNORE BARREL
I don't know the word "ignore."
Just walk past it. Go NORTH.
PUZZLE 009
The Cellar Spider companion puzzle
Cellar. A large spider sits in a web. It regards you with what you can only describe as professional skepticism.
KILL SPIDER, HIT SPIDER, ATTACK SPIDER, AVOID SPIDER, IGNORE SPIDER, TAKE SPIDER
NOTE: The spider becomes your companion if you compliment it. This is the only time in the game the COMPLIMENT verb is used. It is used here, and only here.
COMPLIMENT SPIDER
You tell the spider it has lovely eyes. All eight of them. The spider descends and sits on your shoulder. You have gained a spider companion. Its name is Gerald.
AREA 1 COMPLETE
Score: 12/400
Moves: ~28
Gerald: Present

Area 2: The Forest Path

Puzzles 10–17
PUZZLE 010
The Troll Bridge — Toll currency puzzle
You stand before a bridge. A troll blocks your path. "Pay the toll," it says. "One coin."
PAY TROLL, GIVE COIN TO TROLL, BRIBE TROLL, FIGHT TROLL, SNEAK PAST TROLL
NOTE: You have no coin. The game has never given you a coin. You must make one. Examine the brass key. It is described as "nearly coin-shaped." The game accepts FLATTEN KEY as a verb to make a makeshift coin. You need the rock from two screens back that was also not mentioned in any room description.
GO BACK EXAMINE GROUND
You notice a rock. It was here the whole time.
TAKE ROCK GO SOUTH FLATTEN KEY WITH ROCK
You hammer the key into a rough disc shape. It resembles a coin if you squint.
GIVE COIN TO TROLL
The troll bites it, shrugs, and steps aside.
NOTE: You have now destroyed the key. This is fine. The lock from Puzzle 22 can also be opened by telling the lock a joke. Specifically, the joke must be "WHY DID THE SKELETON GO TO THE PARTY ALONE? BECAUSE HE HAD NO BODY TO GO WITH." I am not making this up.
PUZZLE 011
The Troll Bridge — The Troll's Question trivia puzzle
The troll takes your coin then raises a hand. "One more thing. Answer me this: what is the airspeed velocity of an unladen swallow?"
AFRICAN OR EUROPEAN, ELEVEN METERS PER SECOND, I DON'T KNOW
NOTE: The answer is not the Monty Python reference. The answer is not any real measurement. The answer the game accepts is THURSDAY. No explanation is given. THURSDAY.
SAY THURSDAY
The troll nods sagely. "Correct," it says. "None may challenge the Thursday." You may cross.
PUZZLE 012
The Enchanted Tree music puzzle
A vast oak stands in a clearing. Its trunk has a face. The face looks annoyed.
TALK TO TREE, ASK TREE, EXAMINE TREE, CLIMB TREE, HUG TREE
NOTE: The tree wants music. You have the silver whistle. However, you cannot BLOW WHISTLE or PLAY WHISTLE. You must TOOT WHISTLE. Only TOOT. The tree is very particular about terminology.
TOOT WHISTLE
You toot the whistle. A single, reedy note fills the air. The tree's face breaks into a reluctant smile. "Fine," it says. "You may have this." A branch lowers, offering a golden acorn.
TAKE ACORN
You take the golden acorn. It hums quietly.
PUZZLE 013
The Invisible Wall parser issue
Forest path. All exits seem blocked. You have tried every direction.
GO NORTH, GO SOUTH, GO EAST, GO WEST, GO UP, GO DOWN, GO IN, GO OUT
NOTE: The exit is NORTHEAST. The game has not used diagonal directions anywhere before this point and does not use them anywhere after. NORTHEAST. Just this once.
GO NORTHEAST
You push through some branches and find yourself on a winding path.
PUZZLE 014
The Pond — The Fish animal interaction
A pond reflects the grey sky. A fish bobs near the surface. It appears to be reading a very small newspaper.
TAKE FISH, CATCH FISH, TALK TO FISH, EXAMINE FISH, READ NEWSPAPER
NOTE: Gerald the spider (from Puzzle 9) is required here. If you do not have Gerald, you cannot proceed and the game will not tell you why. Gerald catches the fish for you if you command him.
GERALD, CATCH FISH
Gerald leaps from your shoulder with surprising athletic form and returns with a small, indignant fish. The fish has dropped its newspaper.
TAKE NEWSPAPER
You take the tiny fish newspaper. The headline reads "LOCAL HERO STRUGGLES WITH BASIC TASKS."
NOTE: Keep the newspaper. Puzzle 41. Don't ask.
PUZZLE 015
The Witch's Hut — Entry etiquette puzzle
A hut made of suspiciously brown materials sits among the trees. Smoke rises from the chimney.
KNOCK DOOR, OPEN DOOR, ENTER HUT, CALL OUT
You tried to enter without knocking. The door turned into a mouth and ate you.
NOTE: You must knock AND wait for three turns AND say "good morning" even if it is not morning. The game tracks none of these conditions visibly. You will just get "nothing happens" until all three are satisfied.
KNOCK
You knock.
WAIT WAIT WAIT SAY GOOD MORNING
The door opens. An ancient witch peers out. "Well," she says, "at least you have manners."
PUZZLE 016
The Witch's Trade inventory puzzle
Witch's hut interior. The witch wants something. She doesn't say what.
ASK WITCH WHAT SHE WANTS, GIVE FISH, GIVE ACORN, GIVE WHISTLE, GIVE CANDLE, GIVE MAT
NOTE: She wants the fish. But only if you have first READ the fish's newspaper. If you haven't read it, GIVE FISH produces "She wrinkles her nose." If you have read it, she takes the fish and gives you a potion. The newspaper reading unlocks a hidden flag with absolutely no foreshadowing.
READ NEWSPAPER
You read about local goings-on. A barn dance. A missing cow. Your own struggles, reported without sympathy.
GIVE FISH TO WITCH
The witch takes the fish with a knowing smile. "Ah, an informed visitor." She hands you a small blue potion.
TAKE POTION
PUZZLE 017
The Forest Maze mapping puzzle
A maze of twisty little paths, all alike. You will be here for a while.
NOTE: The maze is 7×7 rooms. The correct path through is: N, N, E, S, E, E, N, N, W, N, E, N. If you deviate, you loop. There are no landmarks. The game's hint system, which costs $2.99/minute on the 1-900 number listed in the manual, does not cover the maze ("technical issue"). Draw a map. I'm sorry.
NNESEENNWNEN
You emerge from the forest. A stone tower looms before you.
AREA 2 COMPLETE
Score: 67/400
Moves: ~85
Sanity: Questionable

Area 3: The Stone Tower

Puzzles 18–25
PUZZLE 018
The Tower Door — A Familiar Problem callbacks
A massive iron door blocks the tower entrance.
OPEN DOOR, VISIT DOOR, PUSH DOOR, BREAK DOOR, KNOCK DOOR
NOTE: Not VISIT this time. Also not OPEN. The verb is SALUTE. You must salute the door. The door has a faint heraldic crest. This is the only visual cue and it requires EXAMINE DOOR first.
EXAMINE DOOR
The door bears a faded military crest. It seems to demand respect.
SALUTE DOOR
You snap a crisp salute. The door swings open with a creak of approval.
PUZZLE 019
The Guard Skeleton joke puzzle
Tower ground floor. A skeleton guard blocks the staircase. It rattles its bones menacingly.
FIGHT SKELETON, ATTACK SKELETON, PUSH SKELETON, BRIBE SKELETON, TALK TO SKELETON, USE BLUE POTION ON SKELETON
NOTE: Remember the lock joke from Puzzle 10's note? It works here too. You must tell the skeleton the specific skeleton joke. This is the game's single most absurd puzzle and I say that having just saluted a door.
TELL SKELETON JOKE
I don't understand "tell skeleton joke." Try being more specific.
SAY WHY DID THE SKELETON GO TO THE PARTY ALONE BECAUSE HE HAD NO BODY TO GO WITH
The skeleton pauses. Its jaw drops (literally). It begins to rattle uncontrollably — laughter. It steps aside, still chuckling. Bits of it fall off.
PUZZLE 020
The Crumbling Staircase weight puzzle
The staircase looks unstable. Each step is labelled with a maximum weight.
GO UP, CLIMB STAIRS, RUN UP, JUMP UP
The stairs collapse. You fall. You are dead. (This happens on every attempt if you're carrying too much.)
NOTE: The game has an invisible weight system it has never mentioned. You must DROP the rock before ascending. The rock was the only item you might naturally have dropped already, in which case you still have it because you're a hoarder like me, and now you must drop it here.
DROP ROCK
You leave the rock at the base of the stairs.
CLIMB STAIRS
You ascend carefully. The stairs groan but hold.
PUZZLE 021
The Locked Library Door verb recall
Tower second floor. A door labeled LIBRARY is locked.
NOTE: No key. You destroyed it. The joke works here. You do not need to type the full joke. Just SAY SKELETON JOKE and the game now recognises it as a phrase since you used it in Puzzle 19. This is the only time the game demonstrates memory of a prior puzzle solution.
SAY SKELETON JOKE
You tell the skeleton joke to the door. The door does not laugh. But the lock, which apparently has a sense of humour, clicks open.
PUZZLE 022
The Library — The Floating Book magic item
A dusty library. Books line the walls. One book floats in the centre of the room at eye level, glowing faintly.
READ BOOK, TAKE BOOK, OPEN BOOK, GRAB BOOK, REACH FOR BOOK
You grabbed the book with bare hands. It exploded. You are dead.
NOTE: You must use the welcome mat (from Puzzle 2!) as oven mitts to take the book safely. If you do not have the mat, start over. I said this at Puzzle 2. I told you.
WEAR MAT
You fashion the mat into crude gloves. They say "WELCOME" on the palms.
TAKE BOOK
Protected by the mat, you grasp the book. It hums with energy but does not explode.
PUZZLE 023
The Library — Reading the Book learn spell
Library. You hold the glowing book.
READ BOOK
The book contains one spell: FLORBINATE. "To florbinate," the book explains, "is to cause a thing to become slightly more Tuesday." You learn the spell FLORBINATE. You have no idea what this means but you learn it anyway.
NOTE: FLORBINATE is used exactly once, in Puzzle 44. It makes a dragon more Tuesday, which calms it. Do not question this. File it away.
PUZZLE 024
The Tower Roof — The Wind Puzzle environmental
Tower roof. The wind is fierce. Across a chasm is another platform. There is no bridge.
JUMP, JUMP CHASM, FLY, USE POTION, THROW ACORN, BLOW WHISTLE
NOTE: The wind blows east. You must open your inventory, select the welcome mat (still on your hands as gloves), and USE MAT AS SAIL. This treats you as a sail-powered object. You will glide across. No, there is no physical sense in which this is plausible.
USE MAT AS SAIL
You hold your mat-gloves wide. The wind catches them. You are lifted and carried east across the chasm like an extremely reluctant kite.
PUZZLE 025
The Gargoyle Statue item combination
Eastern Platform. A stone gargoyle sits here. Its mouth is open. Inside the mouth: nothing. But there's a slot in the base shaped like an acorn.
PUT ACORN IN SLOT
The acorn fits perfectly. The gargoyle's eyes light up gold. It opens its mouth wider and vomits out a grappling hook with practiced efficiency.
TAKE HOOK
Taken. The gargoyle looks relieved.
AREA 3 COMPLETE
Score: 145/400
Moves: ~140
Gerald: Still present, somehow

Area 4: The Dungeon Proper

Puzzles 26–35
PUZZLE 026
The Dungeon Gate tool use
A gate of iron bars. Beyond it: darkness and the smell of moss.
THROW HOOK AT GATE
The hook catches on the bars.
PULL HOOK
The gate tears free. You enter the dungeon.
PUZZLE 027
The Pit Room timing puzzle
A room with a large pit in the centre. A pendulum swings over the pit. Chains hang from the ceiling.
CROSS PIT, JUMP PIT, USE HOOK ON CHAIN, CLIMB CHAIN, GRAB CHAIN
NOTE: You must wait for the pendulum to swing to the right (EXAMINE PENDULUM to track it), then throw the hook onto the far chain on the exact turn the pendulum is right. The pendulum swings right on turns that are multiples of 3 after you enter the room. Count your moves.
EXAMINE PENDULUM
The pendulum swings left. Then right. It seems to be on a cycle.
WAITWAIT THROW HOOK AT CHAIN
Perfect timing! The hook snags the far chain.
SWING ON HOOK
You swing across the pit. You land on the far side, slightly more heroic than before.
PUZZLE 028
The Prisoner character puzzle
A cell. Inside, a bedraggled prisoner rattles their chains. "Free me," they implore.
FREE PRISONER, BREAK CHAINS, UNLOCK CELL, GIVE POTION TO PRISONER
NOTE: The prisoner is freed by asking Gerald to chew through the chains. Gerald is a spider. Spiders cannot chew through iron. The game does not care about this.
GERALD, CHEW CHAINS
Gerald sets about the chains with what can only be described as ambition. The chains fall away. The prisoner is free.
TALK TO PRISONER
"I am the dungeon's former accountant," the prisoner says. "I know the combination to the Vault: it is 4-8-15-16-23-42." He then walks into a wall and disappears. This is never explained.
PUZZLE 029
The Pressure Plate Floor weight again
The corridor floor is covered in tiles with skulls on them.
CROSS FLOOR, JUMP OVER TILES, WALK CAREFULLY, STEP ON TILES, AVOID TILES
You stepped on a skull tile. Spikes. You are dead.
NOTE: You must step ONLY on the tiles with crossbones (not skulls). The game describes both as "skull tiles" in the room description and only tells them apart when you EXAMINE FLOOR. Do that first.
EXAMINE FLOOR
On closer inspection, some tiles have skulls; others have crossbones. The crossbones tiles seem slightly less threatening.
STEP ON CROSSBONES TILES
You hop carefully across, stepping only on crossbones. You survive. The skulls watch with disappointment.
PUZZLE 030
The Water Room blue potion
The corridor is flooded knee-deep. You cannot proceed without dealing with it.
NOTE: The blue potion from the witch drains water when poured. Not into the water — onto the wall. You pour it on the WALL. The wall absorbs the water. This is the potion's use. The potion's label says nothing. The witch said nothing. There is no clue.
POUR POTION ON WALL
The potion hits the wall. The wall glows faintly blue. The water drains away into the stonework with a satisfied slurp.
PUZZLE 031
The Many-Faced Door riddle puzzle
A door with seven faces carved into it. Each face asks a question simultaneously. It is very loud in here.
NOTE: You must answer each face in order by EXAMINEing them individually (EXAMINE FIRST FACE, EXAMINE SECOND FACE, etc.). Each asks a riddle. The answers are: SHADOW, A MAP, AN EGG, SILENCE, TOMORROW, A PROMISE, and THURSDAY again. Yes. Thursday again.
EXAMINE FIRST FACESAY SHADOW EXAMINE SECOND FACESAY A MAP EXAMINE THIRD FACESAY AN EGG EXAMINE FOURTH FACESAY SILENCE EXAMINE FIFTH FACESAY TOMORROW EXAMINE SIXTH FACESAY A PROMISE EXAMINE SEVENTH FACESAY THURSDAY
All seven faces smile. The door sighs with satisfaction and opens.
PUZZLE 032
The Trapped Chest (Again) trap
A chest. It looks exactly like the one in the cottage.
SET CLOCK, OPEN CHEST
There is no clock here. You opened the chest without setting a non-existent clock to 3:00. Needles. You are dead.
NOTE: EXAMINE CHEST reveals a tiny clock face embedded in the lid. You must SAY THREE at the chest. Then open it. I know. I know.
EXAMINE CHEST
A small clock face is embedded in the lid. It appears to be waiting for input.
SAY THREE
The chest hums. The clock hands spin to 3:00.
OPEN CHEST
Inside: a purple gem and a note reading "NICE TRY." You take neither; you take the gem anyway.
TAKE GEM
PUZZLE 033
The Underground Lake navigation
A vast underground lake. A rowboat is tied to a dock. The far shore is barely visible.
GET IN BOAT, ENTER BOAT, TAKE BOAT, ROW BOAT, USE BOAT
NOTE: The boat is tied. UNTIE BOAT first. Then ENTER BOAT. Then ROW BOAT NORTH. In exactly that sequence. Skipping UNTIE will cause you to drag the dock and the dock to collapse and you to drown. There are no oars mentioned. The game gives you invisible oars upon ENTER BOAT.
UNTIE BOAT ENTER BOAT
You step into the boat. Somehow, oars appear. You choose not to question this.
ROW BOAT NORTH
You row across the underground lake. Something large moves beneath you. It does not attack. It just... observes.
PUZZLE 034
The Island Shrine offering puzzle
A small island. A shrine stands here. An altar has an indentation shaped like a gem.
PUT GEM IN ALTAR
The gem fits. The shrine glows. A hidden compartment opens, revealing a silver sword.
TAKE SWORD
You take the silver sword. You feel significantly more equipped to deal with whatever comes next. This feeling is somewhat premature.
PUZZLE 035
The Vault Door combination
A massive vault door with a combination dial.
NOTE: Use the accountant's combination from Puzzle 28: 4-8-15-16-23-42. Input each number separately.
TURN DIAL TO 4TURN DIAL TO 8TURN DIAL TO 15 TURN DIAL TO 16TURN DIAL TO 23TURN DIAL TO 42
The vault door opens with a triumphant thunk.
AREA 4 COMPLETE
Score: 240/400
Moves: ~230
Sword: Acquired

Area 5: The Inner Sanctum

Puzzles 36–43
PUZZLE 036
The Hall of Mirrors reflection puzzle
An endless corridor of mirrors. You can see yourself reflected infinitely. One reflection is wrong.
NOTE: EXAMINE each mirror (FIRST MIRROR through TENTH MIRROR). The seventh mirror shows you without Gerald. Gerald should be on your shoulder. BREAK SEVENTH MIRROR.
EXAMINE SEVENTH MIRROR
Your reflection appears, but Gerald is absent. Something is wrong here.
BREAK SEVENTH MIRROR
The mirror shatters. A small demon tumbles out, screeches, and vanishes. A door is now visible behind where the mirror stood.
PUZZLE 037
The Welcome Mat — Its True Purpose callback payoff
A bare stone room. A door marked EXIT is on the north wall. The floor before it is covered in broken glass.
NOTE: The mat. The welcome mat you have been carrying since Puzzle 2, fashioned into gloves in Puzzle 22, used as a sail in Puzzle 24... now DROP MAT and it becomes a path across the glass. It reassembles itself into a flat mat upon being dropped. The game treats inventory state as fluid. You just roll with it at this point.
DROP MAT
The mat unfolds itself into a perfect rectangle and lies flat across the glass. It says "WELCOME" in green lettering.
WALK ACROSS MAT
You walk across the mat. The glass crunches harmlessly beneath it. You reach the door.
PUZZLE 038
The Whispering Gallery audio puzzle
A circular room. Strange whispers echo from the walls. You feel watched.
LISTEN, EXAMINE WALLS, SEARCH ROOM, SAY ANYTHING
NOTE: The whispers are saying the word "HUSH." You must HUSH. Just type HUSH. The walls quiet. A passage opens. There is genuinely no way to know this without trial and error or this walkthrough.
HUSH
You say "hush." The whispers stop, as if embarrassed. A passage opens in the east wall.
PUZZLE 039
The Illusory Floor faith puzzle
The passage ends at a vast drop. There appears to be no floor. The opposite side is 20 feet away.
JUMP, USE HOOK, THROW HOOK, FLY, GO NORTH
NOTE: The floor is there. It's invisible. You must STEP FORWARD. The game was inspired by Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade and is not subtle about it. Just walk forward.
STEP FORWARD
Your foot finds invisible stone. A bridge! It was here all along. You walk across with growing confidence and diminishing dignity.
PUZZLE 040
The Golem Guardian weakness puzzle
A massive stone golem blocks the way. It is enormous. Your sword does nothing — the text says "The sword bounces off harmlessly" no matter what you do.
ATTACK GOLEM, HIT GOLEM, USE SWORD, SLASH GOLEM, STAB GOLEM, FLORBINATE GOLEM
NOTE: The golem is defeated by telling it a secret. Any secret. The verb is CONFESS. You confess to the golem. The golem, moved by your vulnerability, steps aside. I cannot defend this puzzle.
CONFESS TO GOLEM
You quietly confess that you've been afraid the whole time and haven't known what you're doing. The golem's stone face softens. It steps aside. One tear rolls down its cheek, which is geologically improbable.
PUZZLE 041
The Newspaper Stand currency again
A newspaper stand in the dungeon. A vendor, seemingly unbothered by their location, stands behind it.
NOTE: The vendor wants to trade. They want "news from outside." You have the fish's tiny newspaper. This is Puzzle 14's payoff, 27 puzzles later. Give the tiny newspaper to the vendor.
GIVE NEWSPAPER TO VENDOR
The vendor's eyes light up. "Ah! The barn dance! The missing cow!" They hand you a torch and a map fragment in exchange. "Slow news week otherwise," they add.
TAKE TORCHTAKE MAP FRAGMENT
PUZZLE 042
The Dark Corridor (Again, Worse) light source
Total darkness. The candle went out fifteen rooms ago when you put it down to examine something. The torch is unlit.
LIGHT TORCH, SNEEZE ON TORCH, USE TORCH
NOTE: You cannot sneeze here. Sneezing only works in the cottage. The torch is lit by rubbing it against the wall exactly three times. RUB TORCH ON WALL. Three times.
RUB TORCH ON WALL
Some sparks, no flame.
RUB TORCH ON WALL
More sparks.
RUB TORCH ON WALL
The torch catches. The corridor is illuminated. You can see three doors and what appears to be a motivational poster. It reads "HANG IN THERE." There is a skeleton hanging from it.
PUZZLE 043
The Three Doors logic puzzle
Three identical doors: LEFT, MIDDLE, RIGHT. A sign says one leads forward, one leads back to the start, one leads to instant death.
OPEN LEFT DOOR, OPEN MIDDLE DOOR, OPEN RIGHT DOOR, READ MAP FRAGMENT
NOTE: The map fragment shows an X on the right door. But the map is mirrored. Read the map in a mirror. You must EXAMINE MAP IN MIRROR. There are no mirrors here. You must REMEMBER THE HALL OF MIRRORS and specifically, you should have taken a SHARD of the broken seventh mirror from Puzzle 36. If you didn't, the game allows EXAMINE MAP BACKWARDS as an alternative. The answer is LEFT door.
EXAMINE MAP BACKWARDS
Held up to a notional mirror, the X marks the left door.
OPEN LEFT DOOR
The left door opens onto a grand staircase leading upward. You were right. Somehow.
AREA 5 COMPLETE
Score: 315/400
Moves: ~310
Gerald: Still here. Absolute legend.

Area 6: The Final Confrontation

Puzzles 44–50
PUZZLE 044
The Dragon FLORBINATE
A dragon the size of a house fills the chamber. It is furious. It is breathing fire. Your sword is melting in your hands.
ATTACK DRAGON, KILL DRAGON, USE SWORD, FLEE, TALK TO DRAGON, GIVE DRAGON ANYTHING
You tried to fight. The dragon ate you. It barely noticed.
NOTE: FLORBINATE. From Puzzle 23. The spell you learned from the floating book. Cast it on the dragon.
FLORBINATE DRAGON
You cast the spell. The dragon pauses mid-roar. It blinks. Its scales shift to a slightly more Tuesday hue. "Oh," the dragon says, in a voice like boulders. "Oh, it is a bit Tuesday, isn't it." The dragon settles down and falls into a calm, philosophical mood. It gestures for you to pass.
PUZZLE 045
The Dragon's Riddle (It Has One More Thing to Say) bonus riddle
The Tuesday Dragon stops you. "One question," it says, reflectively. "On a Tuesday, what is the most Tuesday thing?"
SAY THURSDAY
The dragon tilts its head. "...hm. Fair enough," it says, and moves aside entirely. You walk past a dragon that is now contemplating Thursday.
PUZZLE 046
The Wizard's Chamber — The Wizard final boss intro
You enter the wizard's chamber. An ancient, bearded wizard sits at a desk, doing paperwork. He looks up with mild annoyance.
ATTACK WIZARD, TALK TO WIZARD, DEFEAT WIZARD, THREATEN WIZARD, USE SWORD
NOTE: The wizard is not a boss fight. He is tired. He has been doing this for centuries. You must HELP WIZARD with his paperwork. This is not a metaphor. You literally fill in a form with him. The game asks you three bureaucratic questions.
HELP WIZARD
The wizard looks surprised, then grateful. "Name of adventurer?" he asks.
SAY YOUR NAME
"Purpose of dungeon visit?"
SAY HEROIC QUEST
"Method of entrance?"
SAY THROUGH THE FRONT DOOR
The wizard stamps the form. "Right. Everything checks out. The treasure's through there." He points north. "Tea?" he adds.
PUZZLE 047
The Wizard's Tea optional but important
The wizard is offering tea. You don't have to take it. You absolutely should take it.
NOTE: Accepting the tea grants 50 points and changes the ending. Refusing grants 5 points and gives you the "Lesser Ending." The game mentions none of this. Always take the tea.
TAKE TEA
You accept the tea. It is, improbably, perfect. You drink it. The wizard nods. There is a moment of genuine peace. It is the best moment in the game.
PUZZLE 048
The Treasure Room — The Final Lock combination callback
The treasure room. A chest on a pedestal. A combination lock. Four digits.
NOTE: The combination is 3000. Why? Because you set two clocks to 3:00, each with four digits (0300), and 0300 + 0300 = something something the game is broken it's just 3000. Enter it.
ENTER 3000
The lock opens. The chest opens. Light pours out.
TAKE TREASURE
You take the treasure. It is, after all you have been through, a slightly nicer version of the golden acorn you gave to the gargoyle. The universe is funny like that.
PUZZLE 049
Gerald's Farewell emotional
As you prepare to leave, Gerald crawls from your shoulder and faces you.
NOTE: If you SAY GOODBYE TO GERALD, you receive 25 bonus points and the game acknowledges the journey. If you just leave, Gerald silently descends. The game tracks whether you said goodbye. It matters for the score.
SAY GOODBYE TO GERALD
Gerald raises two of his eight legs in a solemn wave. He has been with you through trolls, witches, filing cabinets, and a Tuesday dragon. He descends the wall and disappears into the dark. The walkthrough author needed a moment after this. He's fine. He's fine.
PUZZLE 050
The Exit — Going Home finale
The dungeon entrance. Freedom is one step away. The sun is visible through the doorway.
GO OUT, EXIT, LEAVE, GO NORTH, STEP OUTSIDE

GO HOME
You step out of the dungeon into the afternoon light.
